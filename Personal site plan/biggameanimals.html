What do you do after you kill your animal?

Remember, shooting big game animals are just the beginning. After shooting big game critters, you still need to track it down to ensure the kill and find the carcass. To do that, you will need to follow blood trails to find them. Then you must drag it out of the forest and get it processed. Whether you’re taking it to the butcher or the taxidermist, the real work comes after you shoot the animal. While some hunters take pride in packing out their own kills, you don’t have to be stuck with all that hard work after the thrill. When you book a guided hunt, like the big game packages you can get at West Canyon Ranch, they take care of transporting your kill for you.

What happens if you shoot the wrong animal by accident?

If a hunter accidentally kills the wrong animal, the consequences can vary depending on the circumstances and the laws of the region where the hunting took place. Generally, the hunter is expected to report the incident to local wildlife authorities immediately. An impatient hunter who fails to make sure his target is clear could also unintentionally kill two big game animals with one rifle shot. Failure to report an accident like that can lead to poaching charges that carry severe penalties.
